DIY PC

KNOW YOUR STEPS

1. Pick your Processor
info The processor is the heart of the laptop and has the greatest impact on the laptop's productivity. A faster processor means apps load quickly, you can run multiple apps at once, and the computer won't lag and cause slowdowns when you run processor-intensive tasks. Larger the value of TDP(Thermal Design Power) and core, better the performance is.

2. Choose your Motherboard
info The motherboard is the backbone that ties the computerâ€™s components together at one spot and allows them to interact with each other. Without it, none of the computer pieces, such as the processor, GPU, etc could interact. Thus a motherboard has to be compatible with the processor and RAM. Since only the compatible components of the chosen components are displayed, it won't be an issue.

3. Choose your RAM
info RAM(Random Access Memory) works as our PC's active working memory. The more RAM, the more information a computer can work with at the same time, so it can have a dramatic effect on the total performance of the system.

4. Pick your GPU or Skip
info GPU(Graphics processing unit) is a specialized processor designed to accelerate graphics rendering. GPUs can process many pieces of data simultaneously, making them useful for machine learning, video editing, gaming applications, etc. Having a GPU is not mandatory because a PC can function well even without it.

5. Pick your Storage
info Storage component is of two types namely SSD(solid-state drive) HDD (hard disk drive). SSD is faster than HDD. SSDs don't have physical moving parts. More the memory, more data it can store. While using SSD, the laptop will boot quickly.
